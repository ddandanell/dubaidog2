Build a professional Dubai pet relocation website.

This is not a cute pet website. It should look like a premium logistics and relocation company that specializes in dogs and cats moving to and from Dubai.

The website must feel serious, clear, fast, trustworthy, and price-aware.

The main idea:
We help pet owners move dogs and cats to or from Dubai by checking the route, documents, airline, crate, breed risk, timeline, and cost before the pet travels.

The customer is usually stressed, confused, and worried about making a mistake. They want to know:
- Can my pet travel?
- What documents do I need?
- What will it cost?
- Which airline can take my pet?
- How long does it take?
- What happens if something goes wrong?
- Can someone handle everything for me?

The website should answer those questions fast.

Main navigation and footer:
- Home
- Relocation Service
- Worldwide Relocation
- Pricing Calculator
- About Us
- Contact

Do not put too many pages in the top navigation. Keep the navigation simple. The deeper SEO pages and support pages should appear inside the page body as cards, related links, service sections, calculators, FAQs, and CTAs.

Website structure:

Home
The front page should route visitors into the right path:
1. Moving a pet into Dubai
2. Moving a pet out of Dubai
3. Emergency or complicated case
4. Get a price estimate
5. Check documents

Relocation Service
This page explains the main services:
- Dog import to Dubai
- Dog export from Dubai
- Emergency pet relocation

Worldwide Relocation
This page explains route-based relocation:
- Dubai to UK
- Dubai to Europe
- Dubai to USA

Pricing Calculator
This page helps users estimate cost based on:
- Origin
- Destination
- Pet type
- Breed
- Weight
- Travel date
- Document status
- Crate needs
- Pickup or delivery
- Urgency

About Us
This page builds trust:
- How we work
- Our process
- Why trust us

Contact
This page should not just be a basic form. It should be a case intake system:
- Request a quote
- Need help
- Emergency help

Homepage sections:
1. Hero section
Headline: Pet Relocation in Dubai, Handled Properly From Start to Finish
Subheadline: Import, export, documents, flights, crate planning, airport clearance, and door-to-door coordination for dogs and cats moving to or from Dubai.
Buttons: Get relocation estimate, Check my documents, Emergency help

2. Trust bar
Show short trust points:
- Dubai import and export support
- Flight and cargo coordination
- Vet document guidance
- Door-to-door planning
- Emergency case review

3. Main service doors
Show three large cards:
- Moving a pet into Dubai
- Moving a pet out of Dubai
- Emergency or complicated case

4. Price calculator preview
Show that users can estimate price based on route, breed, weight, documents, airline, crate, and urgency.

5. How it works
Show a simple 6-step process:
- Pet and route review
- Document and vaccine check
- Airline and crate planning
- Permit and certificate coordination
- Flight and airport handling
- Delivery and final handover

6. Dubai rules and documents
Explain that pet relocation is not guesswork. It depends on correct documents, microchip, vaccines, health certificates, airline rules, and timing.

7. Popular routes
Show only a few at first:
- Dubai to UK
- Dubai to Europe
- Dubai to USA
- Worldwide to Dubai

8. Airline and airport handling
Explain cargo, hold travel, crate requirements, transit planning, and airport clearance.

9. Breed and risk review
Do not say “illegal dogs.” Use professional language:
Restricted Breeds and High-Risk Travel Cases
Include:
- Restricted breeds
- Snub-nose dogs
- Large dogs
- Senior dogs
- Puppies
- Multiple pets

10. Why choose us
Show:
- Clear relocation plan
- Transparent cost structure
- Document-first process
- Airline-aware planning
- Fast case review
- Door-to-door coordination
- Emergency support

11. Customer profiles
Show that the service is for:
- Expats moving to Dubai
- Expats leaving Dubai
- Families with dogs
- Corporate relocation clients
- High-income villa and apartment residents
- Owners with urgent cases
- Owners with restricted or difficult breeds

12. FAQ
Answer common questions about cost, documents, airlines, restricted breeds, cargo, timing, and emergency help.

13. Final CTA
End with:
Start With a Case Review
Tell us where your pet is going, what documents you have, and when you need to travel. We will check the route, risks, timeline, and likely cost before you move forward.

Tone:
Professional, calm, direct, premium, service-driven, international, fast, and clear.

Avoid:
- Cute pet-shop language
- Cartoon paw graphics
- Cheap-looking design
- Too much emotional text
- Too many vague promises
- Saying “we love pets” too much without proving process and competence

Color style:
Use a premium Dubai logistics look:
- Deep navy
- Charcoal
- Warm white
- Sand gold
- Copper gold
- Soft grey
- Amber for warning/risk
- Green for approved/check status

Visual style:
Use realistic premium images:
- Calm dog beside travel crate
- Professional handler
- Dubai skyline
- Airport or cargo feeling
- Owner with documents
- Clean villa or apartment setting
- Pet crate with travel label
- Professional pickup or handover

Avoid:
- Random dog stock photos
- Cartoon animals
- Cheap courier look
- Messy airport images
- Overly playful pet-shop design

Core positioning:
Dubai pet relocation without airport surprises.

Alternative positioning:
Pet relocation planned properly before anything moves.

Main brand idea:
Check before you move.

Everything on the site should connect back to this:
- Check the route
- Check the documents
- Check the airline
- Check the breed
- Check the crate
- Check the timeline
- Check the cost

The website should work like a professional pet relocation control system. It should make the customer feel that the company understands the full process and can handle the move properly.
